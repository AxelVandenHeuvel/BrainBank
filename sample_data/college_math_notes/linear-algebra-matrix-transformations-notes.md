# Linear Algebra - Matrix Transformations Notes

## Class takeaway
Matrices are not just arrays of numbers. In this course they represent Linear Transformations, which means every matrix question is really asking what happens to vectors under a rule that preserves addition and scalar multiplication.

## Core facts
- Determinants tell me whether a matrix squashes space to a lower dimension and whether orientation flips.
- Eigenvalues describe stretch factors along special directions.
- If a matrix has determinant zero, the transformation is not invertible.

## Visual intuition
Our professor drew the unit square and showed how Matrices turn it into a parallelogram. The area scaling matched the Determinants exactly, which finally made that formula feel useful instead of random. Eigenvalues make more sense when I imagine vectors that do not rotate away from their own line.

## Study note
When I am given a Linear Transformations question on the exam, I should test the standard basis vectors first. Their images become the columns of the matrix, and that is way faster than trying to guess the matrix all at once.
