# Calculus I - Derivative Rules Study Guide

## Big picture
Derivatives measure instantaneous change, which is why they keep showing up in every Calculus homework set. The slope of the tangent line is just the derivative evaluated at that point, so every graph problem can be translated into a rate-of-change problem.

## Rules I need memorized
- Power rule: d/dx of x^n is n * x^(n-1)
- Product Rule: differentiate the first, leave the second, then add first times derivative of the second
- Chain Rule: take the derivative of the outer function and multiply by the derivative of the inner function

## Example that tripped me up
For y = (3x^2 + 1)(x^3 - 4), I forgot the Product Rule on the practice quiz and lost four points. If the function is composite, like sin(x^2 + 1), I also need the Chain Rule. That one is where I keep dropping the derivative of the inside.

## Exam reminder
I should rewrite radicals as powers before finding Derivatives. It makes the algebra cleaner and keeps me from mixing up the Product Rule and Chain Rule. If the professor asks for a tangent line, I need both the derivative and the original function value at the same x-coordinate.
