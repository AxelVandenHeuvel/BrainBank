# Differential Equations - Separable Models Notes

## Overview
Differential Equations feel less scary once I recognize the pattern. If the y terms and x terms can be pulled to opposite sides, then Separation of Variables is usually the cleanest method.

## Standard models
- Exponential Growth appears when the rate is proportional to the current amount.
- The Logistic Model corrects Exponential Growth by adding a carrying capacity.
- Initial conditions are what turn a family of solutions into one specific answer.

## Example from homework
For dy/dt = ky, dividing by y and integrating gives ln|y| = kt + C, which leads to the familiar Exponential Growth formula after exponentiating. The Logistic Model looks similar at first, but the extra factor (1 - y / L) changes the long-term behavior completely.

## What I need to remember
If a problem says "separable," I should physically separate the variables before integrating. Differential Equations questions are easy to overcomplicate when I start thinking about integrating factors too soon.
