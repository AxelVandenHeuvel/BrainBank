# Calculus II - Integration Techniques Cheat Sheet

## Why this unit matters
Integrals feel like the reverse side of Derivatives, but the class keeps reminding us that area is the most intuitive way to think about them. Riemann Sums are the bridge: rectangles approximate the area first, then Integrals take the limit as the rectangle widths shrink.

## Techniques to recognize quickly
- u-Substitution works when a composition has an inside function whose derivative is almost present.
- The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus lets me connect accumulation functions to Derivatives.
- Definite Integrals are often easier if I simplify the integrand before doing any substitution.

## Example
For integral of 2x * cos(x^2) dx, let u = x^2 so du = 2x dx. Then the problem becomes integral of cos(u) du, which is much easier. I need to slow down on back-substitution because that is where silly errors happen.

## Things to remember for the next quiz
If the question starts with a sum sigma notation and equal-width intervals, it is probably a disguised Riemann Sums problem. If it asks how antiderivatives and area are related, I should quote the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus instead of trying to derive it from scratch.
