# Probability - Midterm Review Notes

## Main idea
Probability is basically a language for uncertainty, but the professor wants us to think in terms of models instead of isolated formulas. Random Variables turn outcomes into numbers, which is why they show up in every chapter even when the story changes.

## Must-know formulas
- Expected Value is the weighted average of possible outcomes.
- Bayes Theorem updates a prior belief after seeing new evidence.
- Independence means the probability of both events factors into the product of their individual probabilities.

## Example from lecture
The medical test example made Bayes Theorem much more intuitive. Even a "good" test can produce a lot of false positives when the condition is rare, so the prior Probability matters just as much as the sensitivity.
